Background

I received my PhD from UCLA in Biomedical Engineering with a specialization in Medical Informatics. Recently, I started as a Research Fellow at Mayo Clinic in Phoenix, AZ as part of the Precision Neurotherapeutics Lab. Currently, my post-doctoral time at Mayo Clinic is focused around expanding my skills in data science, machine learning, and programming.

My dissertation focused on the external validation of survival models in brain cancer using simulation and causal techniques to classify model performance. My primary research interests include predictive disease modeling, medical data integration, machine learning, and external validation.

While at UCLA, I also developed a tablet-based patient surveying system in collaboration with UCLA Family Medicine and the UCLA Wireless Health Institute. The EMMA survey system was used for QUIT (Quit Using Drugs Intervention Trial), a randomized controlled trial targeting interventions in risky drug use for underserved populations of Los Angeles. While working at the National Institutes of Health as a post-baccalaureate researcher, I studied MR imaging techniques and brain atlas development in stroke research.
