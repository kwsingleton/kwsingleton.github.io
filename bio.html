Biography

Kyle is a recent graduate from the University of California, Los Angeles with a PhD in Biomedical Engineering. He received his Bachelor’s degree in Biomedical Engineering from the University of Virginia in 2006. He worked from 2006-2008 with the National Institutes of Health as a post-baccalaureate researcher, studying MRI imaging techniques and brain atlas development in stroke research.

In 2008, he joined the Bioengineering and Medical Imaging Informatics (MII) groups at UCLA. He developed a tablet-based patient surveying system in collaboration with UCLA Family Medicine and the UCLA Wireless Health Institute used for Project QUIT (Quit Using Drugs Intervention Trial), a randomized controlled trial studying risky drug use. Kyle’s dissertation research focuses on improving the application of predictive models to clinical practice by evaluating model accuracy and calibration to external datasets. In addition, his research explores methods for accurately describing model assumptions and the use of casual graphs with transportability theory to correctly identify the effects of model difference among varying data sources.
